What is tier-1

Tier 1 means a ‘medium level’ of coronavirus infection.

  1. You can see people from different households both indoors and outdoors, but only in groups of up to 6 people
  2. There are no restrictions on travel or use of transport but you should still wear a face covering
  3. There are no restrictions on staying overnight somewhere other than your house.
  4. All shops can open
  5. Restaurants, pubs, cafés and other hospitality venues can be open.

What is tier-2

The second tier is applied in areas with a higher level of infections.

  1. You can see people from different households outside in groups of up to 6 people but you can only meet inside with those in your household or support bubble.
  2. Journeys should be limited where possible, but you can still travel and use transport to go to the shops, work and hospitality venues that are open. You should still wear a face covering.
  3. You can only stay overnight somewhere if it's with those in your household or support bubble.
  4. All shops can open
  5. Pubs and bars can open as long as they are able to serve a substantial meal, restaurants can open but should be table service only. Alcohol can only be served with a substantial meal.

What is tier-3

The third tier means very High Alert.

  1. You should only spend time in your house or garden with the people you live with.
  2. Journeys should be minimised, but you can still travel to go to the shops, works and hospitality venues that are open. You should still wear a face covering.
  3. You can't stay overnight somewhere outside of local area unless needed for work, education or similar.
  4. All shops can open
  5. Hospitality venues are closed, but can remain open for takeaway, drive through and delivery services.

What is tier-4

Tier 4 will be very similar to the national lockdown restrictions, meaning people have been asked to stay at home as much as possible.

  1. People living in Tier 4 can only meet one one person from another household outside in a public space.
  2. Those in Tier 4 areas will not be permitted to travel abroad apart from limited exceptions, such as for work purposes.
  3. Non-essential retail, indoor gyms and leisure facilities in Tier 4 areas will close.
  4. Communal worship will be able to continue to take place in Tier 4 areas.
  5. Support bubbles will remain unaffected as well as the exemptions for separated parents and their children.

What is tier-5

Tier-5 means stay at home!!!

  1. You should stay at home as much as possible.
  2. You should only spend time in your house or garden with the people you live with.
  3. You should only travel if it's essential. You should still wear a face covering.
  4. You can't stay overnight somewhere outside your local area unless essential for work, education or similar.
  5. Non-essential shops will be closed. Essential shops such as supermarkets will remain open.
